# Customer Support Automation - Interview Guide

## Purpose
Understand support managers' pain points, current processes, and requirements for AI automation.

## Background Questions
1. How long have you been in customer support?
2. What's your current role and team size?
3. How many tickets do you handle per week?
4. What's your average daily ticket volume?

## Current Process Questions
5. How do you currently triage and route tickets?
6. What tools do you use today (Zendesk, Intercom, etc.)?
7. What's your current deflection rate (if you track it)?
8. What percentage of tickets are tier-1 vs. tier-2 vs. tier-3?
9. How long does it typically take to resolve a tier-1 ticket?
10. What's your escalation process to engineering/product?

## Pain Point Questions
11. What's the most frustrating part of your job?
12. What takes up most of your time?
13. Where do you see the biggest bottlenecks?
14. What tickets get escalated most frequently?
15. What's your biggest concern about scaling support?

## AI Automation Questions
16. Have you tried any AI automation tools? What worked/didn't work?
17. What concerns do you have about AI automation?
18. What would "perfect" automation look like for you?
19. How do you measure success (CSAT, resolution time, etc.)?
20. What metrics do you report to leadership weekly?

## Feature Questions
21. What features would make this indispensable for your team?
22. How would you want AI responses to be reviewed before sending?
23. What kind of confidence threshold would you trust?
24. How would you want to be notified of AI failures?
25. What integrations would you need (Zendesk, Slack, etc.)?

## Beta Interest
26. Would you be interested in beta testing a tool like this?
27. What would make you willing to try a new tool?
28. What's your timeline for implementing a solution like this?
29. Would you be open to a 30-minute follow-up to demo the MVP?
30. Can I mention your company in my case study (anonymized if needed)?
