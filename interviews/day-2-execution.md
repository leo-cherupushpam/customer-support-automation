# Day 2 Execution Guide

## Objective
Publish LinkedIn post and recruit 10 support managers for interviews

## Time Required: 2 hours

---

## Task 1: Publish LinkedIn Post (30 minutes)

### Step 1: Copy this post
```
🚀 Starting a 6-week Build-in-Public journey: Building an AI Customer Support Automation Platform

Goal: Help support teams deflect 60% of tier-1 tickets while maintaining 4.0+ CSAT

Why this matters:
• 79% of service leaders say AI agents are essential (Salesforce State of Service 2025)
• Currently only 30% of cases are resolved by AI - huge room to improve
• Support managers handle 200-500 tickets/week, but spend <50% of time with customers

What I need:
Looking to interview 10 support managers/customer success leaders to understand:
✓ Their biggest pain points with current support workflows
✓ How they measure success (deflection rate, CSAT, resolution time)
✓ What concerns they have about AI automation
✓ What features would make this indispensable for their team

Who I'm talking to:
• Support Managers at SaaS companies (50-500 employees)
• Customer Success Leaders
• VP/Director of Customer Service

Time commitment: 30 minutes via Zoom/Google Meet
When: Next week (flexible timing)
What you get: Full findings summary + early access to beta

If you're interested, DM me or reply to this post!

#CustomerSupport #AI #BuildInPublic #ProductManagement #SaaS
```

### Step 2: Add a professional headshot or project banner
### Step 3: Publish between 8-10 AM on Tuesday-Thursday (best engagement)
### Step 4: Respond to all comments within 2 hours

---

## Task 2: Send 10 LinkedIn DMs (45 minutes)

### Step 1: Identify 10 targets
Look for:
- Current connections in your network
- People with "Support Manager", "Customer Success", "VP Customer Service" titles
- Alumni from your company/school
- People who've posted about customer support challenges

### Step 2: Send personalized DMs (use template below)

**DM Template:**
```
Hi [First Name],

I noticed your experience in [their field/company] and wanted to reach out.

I'm building an AI customer support automation tool and looking to interview 10 support managers about their pain points and requirements for Week 1 of my 6-week Build-in-Public journey.

Would you be open to a 30-minute chat next week? Happy to share the findings with you.

No pitch, just learning!

Thanks,
[Your Name]
```

### Step 3: Track in scheduling-template.md
- Fill in the "Outreach Tracking Log" table
- Note response status for each person

## Task 4: Set Up Scheduling (30 minutes)

### Option A: Calendly (Recommended)
1. Create free Calendly account
2. Set up 30-minute slots for next week
3. Share link in follow-up DMs

### Option B: Google Forms
1. Create simple form: Name, Company, Role, Email, Available Times
2. Share form link in DMs
3. Manually schedule based on responses

### Option C: Manual Scheduling
1. Share your calendar availability directly
2. People reply with preferred time
3. You send calendar invite

## Common Objections & Responses

### "I'm too busy"
**Response:** "Totally understand! How about a quick 15-minute chat instead? Or I can send you the questions and you can reply via email."

### "What's this about?"
**Response:** "Building an AI customer support automation tool. Looking to understand support managers' pain points before I start building. No pitch, just learning from your experience."

### "I don't have time next week"
**Response:** "No problem! I have availability the following week too. What works better for you?"

### "Can you send questions first?"
**Response:** "Absolutely! Here are the main topics: current support workflow, biggest pain points, AI concerns, success metrics. Would 30 minutes work?"
